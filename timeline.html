📅 Election Timeline

A step-by-step breakdown of how the world's largest democracy votes.

Step 01

Election Announcement

The Election Commission of India (ECI) announces the dates through a press conference.

  • Dates for all phases are declared.
  • Notification is sent to the President/Governor.
  • Official gazette notification is issued.
Step 02

Model Code of Conduct

The MCC comes into effect immediately across the country.

  • No new government schemes can be announced.
  • Restrictions on political speeches and rallies.
  • Official machinery cannot be used for campaigning.
Step 03

Nomination Filing

Candidates file their papers with the Returning Officer of their constituency.

  • Security deposit must be made.
  • Affidavit declaring assets, education, and criminal records.
  • A formal oath is taken by the candidate.
Step 04

Scrutiny & Withdrawal

Election officials check the validity of nominations; candidates can withdraw.

  • Returning officer verifies all documents.
  • Valid nominations are published.
  • Last date for withdrawal is usually 2 days after scrutiny.
Step 05

Campaign Period

Political parties and candidates reach out to voters with their vision.

  • Rallies, door-to-door campaigning, and media ads.
  • Release of Party Manifestos.
  • Campaigning must stop 48 hours before polling ends.
Step 06

Polling Day

Voters visit polling booths to cast their votes using EVMs.

  • Polling starts at 7 or 8 AM.
  • Voters' identities are verified.
  • Indelible ink is applied to the finger.
Step 07

Storage of EVMs

EVMs are sealed and moved to high-security 'Strong Rooms'.

  • Political agents verify the sealing.
  • Strong rooms are guarded 24/7 by security forces.
  • CCTV monitoring and three-layer security.
Step 08

Counting Day

Votes are counted under strict supervision at designated centers.

  • Postal ballots are counted first.
  • EVM counts happen in multiple rounds.
  • VVPAT slips from random booths are verified.
Step 09

Results Declaration

Official results are announced and certificates are issued to winners.

  • ECI updates results in real-time online.
  • Winners are formally notified.
  • The list of elected members is submitted to the President.
Step 10

Government Formation

The majority party or coalition is invited to form the government.

  • Party/Coalition with 272+ seats stakes claim.
  • The Prime Minister is sworn in by the President.
  • The new House is constituted.
