🗳️ Voting Guide

Everything you need to know to cast your vote correctly and confidently.

Who can vote?

To be an eligible voter in India, you must meet the following criteria:

  • Must be a citizen of India.
  • Must be at least 18 years of age on the qualifying date (usually January 1st of the year).
  • Must be an ordinary resident in the constituency where registration is sought.
  • Must not be disqualified to be enrolled as a voter.
  • Non-Resident Indians (NRIs) are eligible to vote in their home constituency.

How to Register?

Registration is the first step toward voting. You can register online or offline.

Online Method

Visit the National Voter's Service Portal (NVSP) or use the Voter Helpline App. Fill out Form 6 and upload your documents.

Offline Method

Visit the Electoral Registration Officer (ERO) or Booth Level Officer (BLO) in your area. Submit a physical copy of Form 6.

Required Documents:

  • Recent passport-sized photograph.
  • Age proof (Birth certificate, Passport, or 10th marksheet).
  • Address proof (Aadhaar card, Utility bill, or Bank passbook).

Step-by-Step Polling Day Process

Once you reach the polling station, follow these steps:

  1. First polling officer checks your name on the voter list and ID proof.
  2. Second polling officer marks your finger with indelible ink and gives you a slip.
  3. Third polling officer takes the slip and ensures you are ready to vote.
  4. Proceed to the voting compartment and press the button on the EVM.
  5. Check the VVPAT window for the printed slip to verify your vote.
